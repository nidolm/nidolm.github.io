Social Love Sunday

Vladimier Juliano


For our Social Love Sunday, we went to Padre Pio's Home for Children. Padre Pio's Home for Children is a residential facility and temporary shelter run by the Sisters, Apostles of Jesus Crucified, a religious institute founded by Father Domenico Labellarte, an Italian priest who was a spiritual son of St. Padre Pio for 26 years. The home is located in Rambutan St., Tugbok Proper, Davao City, Philippines. It serves as an institution-based (or Residential-Based) facility and offers case management and psychosocial intervention services to help uplift the lives of the children under its care

The address of Padre Pio's Home for Children.



During the Social Love Sunday event, Me and Jasper had the opportunity to meet a resilient and inspiring young girl named Joanna, who dreams of becoming a nurse. Joanna also impressed us with her exceptional skills in chess, having achieved a checkmate in less than 20 moves, which is truly impressive. As we engaged in community outreach activities with the children at Padre Pio's, we witnessed the daily challenges that Joanna and her peers face. This experience not only deepened our understanding of the struggles faced by marginalized and unprivileged individuals but also inspired us to take action and make a difference in their lives. The event has left a lasting impression on us, reinforcing the importance of community support and outreach.

Me, Jasper, and some of our Friends with Joanna (red).


The Social Love Sunday event not only allowed us to connect with Joanna and other children at the home but also reinforced the importance of community service. It has inspired us to seek out opportunities to contribute to the well-being of those in need and to spread love and compassion in any way we can. As Mother Teresa said, "Love has to be put into action and that action is service." We are committed to taking a more active role in helping our struggling brothers and sisters in the community, whether through volunteering, fundraising, or simply offering a listening ear. The event has shown us that even small acts of kindness and support can have a profound impact on the lives of others, and we are eager to continue our efforts to create a more just and compassionate society.
